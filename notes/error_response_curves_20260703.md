# MCTS Error-Response Curves — Rungs 6-7 (2026-07-03)

Script: `scripts/bench_error_response.jl` (self-contained; not git-committed by the
run that produced this). Reproduce:

```
julia --threads 8 --project scripts/bench_error_response.jl --num-states 400 --states-1600 400
```

Results serialized to `scratchpad/error_response_results.jls`. Runtime ~2 min for
Rung 6 (400 states, 68 cells) + Rung 7 (NN, 400 states × 3 modes @600 sims).

## Where this sits in the staircase

Rungs 1-3 (`test/test_mcts_identity_staircase.jl`) proved the MCTS **arithmetic is
exact**: `Q == input values` to machine epsilon. The convergence sweep
(`notes/mcts_convergence_sweep_20260703.md`) showed finite-budget **allocation
noise with exact values** (knee at 400 iters). Therefore *any* move error measured
here is caused by the **injected input error interacting with search** — never by a
bug in the search core. Rung 6 maps input-error → move-error; Rung 7 places the
real race NN on that map.

## Theory under test (stated to be falsified)

1. **FRESH value noise** (re-drawn every evaluator call) is **VARIANCE** → MCTS
   averages it out; regret ↓ as sims ↑ (≈ 1/√visits per action).
2. **FROZEN value noise** (deterministic per node, hash-seeded) is **BIAS** — like a
   trained NN's systematic error. At a depth-1 evaluator frontier, iterations
   **cannot** fix it: argmax over consistently-shifted constants stays shifted.
   Regret plateaus vs sims at a level set by ε and the local move-gap distribution.
3. **POLICY-only error** (bad priors, exact values) is **cheap**: corrected by a few
   hundred sims (the uniform-prior sweep is the extreme datapoint — corrected by 400).
4. Therefore for a real NN: sims buy correction of value **variance** and **policy**
   error, but only **DEPTH past the frontier** corrects value **bias**.

## Method

**Eval set** — 400 fixed post-dice mutual-bearoff decision states, generated by
random rollout from race starts (`eval_data/race_starts_tuples_bootstrap_no_eval_no_bo.jls`,
seed 42), filtered to be **non-trivial**: ≥3 legal moves and best–second-best exact
move-gap `< 0.5` raw points (and `> 1e-9`, i.e. not a forced tie). Cached to
`/tmp/error_response_states.jls`.

| stat | value |
|------|-------|
| gap (raw pts) | min 0.000, p25 0.0033, **median 0.0277**, p75 0.0772, max 0.498, mean 0.0643 |
| legal moves | min 3, median 6, max 35 |
| doubles | 96 / 400 (24%) |
| mover=0 | 195 / 400 |
| gammon-live (a side has 15 on board) | 159 / 400 |

The gap median (0.028) and p25 (0.003) matter: bearoff move-gaps are **tiny** — this
is the regime the sweep flagged where few PUCT sims can't resolve the optimum.

**Metric** — *regret* in **raw points**: `exact(best) − exact(chosen)`, ≥ 0, via the
turn-aware `bearoff_turn_value` (handles the doubles mid-turn pitfall). Reported as
mean over the eval set. Also `P(wrong) = P(chosen ∉ exact-argmax set)`. One
BatchedMCTS run per state per cell, fresh tree, cpuct=2.0, γ=1, passthrough chance,
batch_size 8.

**Injectors** (inject in normalized units ε/3 on the /3 evaluator scale; ε reported
in raw points). Noise `g(hash)` = a fixed pool of N(0,1) indexed by a state hash of
`(p0,p1,dice₁,dice₂,remaining_actions,current_player)` — distinct nodes/dice get
independent frozen draws:

- **value-frozen** — evaluator returns `v(s) + (ε/3)·g(hash(child))` at frontier leaves.
- **value-fresh** — `v(s) + (ε/3)·randn()` re-drawn every call.
- **policy-frozen** — root priors = `softmax((exactᵥ + ε·g(state,action)) / τ)`,
  τ = 0.02 raw pts (noiseless softmax mean top-1 prior **0.566** — reasonably sharp);
  values **exact** at the frontier. Implemented by making the evaluator return
  `nothing` at the root so the oracle's noisy priors take effect, exact elsewhere.
- **both-frozen** — value-frozen frontier + policy-frozen root at the same ε.

**Selection** — two rules reported per cell:
- **visit-argmax** (standard AlphaZero temperature-0 move) — *primary*.
- **Q-argmax** (what the tree's *values* say) — isolates whether residual regret is a
  value error or a **visit-allocation / prior-capture** artifact.

**Zero-sim anchor** (frozen-value only) — 1-ply argmax over the corrupted values, the
"no search" reference. Derivation: converged MCTS `Q(a) = exactₙₒᵣₘ[a] + s·(ε/3)·g(childkey)`
with `s = (mover==0 ? +1 : −1)` — the white→mover sign is *constant across actions*
(shown analytically: `player_val` is always child-cp-relative, so the root-mover
conversion sign depends only on the root mover). Terminal children carry **no**
evaluator noise (scored via the reward path), matching real use.

## Rung 6 — the curves (mean regret, raw points, 400 states)

**Baseline ε=0** (exact evaluator, uniform priors) — reproduces the sweep shape:

| sims | 30 | 100 | 400 | 1600 |
|------|----|-----|-----|------|
| regret | 0.01028 | 0.00111 | 0.00002 | 0.00000 |
| P(wrong) | 21.7% | 2.2% | 0.3% | 0.0% |

**Zero-sim (no-search) anchors**, frozen value noise: ε=0.01 → 0.00127, ε=0.03 →
0.00501, ε=0.10 → 0.02331, ε=0.30 → 0.05503.

### value-frozen — plateaus at the zero-sim anchor (BIAS)

| ε \ sims | 30 | 100 | 400 | 1600 | zero-sim |
|----------|----|-----|-----|------|----------|
| 0.01 | 0.01181 | 0.00253 | 0.00127 | 0.00120 | **0.00127** |
| 0.03 | 0.01348 | 0.00554 | 0.00491 | 0.00466 | **0.00501** |
| 0.10 | 0.03277 | 0.02304 | 0.02167 | 0.02168 | **0.02331** |
| 0.30 | 0.06788 | 0.05478 | 0.05307 | 0.05364 | **0.05503** |

Q-argmax is nearly identical (the values *are* the bias). Every row converges to its
zero-sim anchor by ~100-400 sims and **stops** — more sims never push below the
1-ply level. Low-sim regret sits *above* the anchor (allocation noise adds on top of
bias) and decays *down* to it.

### value-fresh — decays monotonically to 0 (VARIANCE)

| ε \ sims | 30 | 100 | 400 | 1600 |
|----------|----|-----|-----|------|
| 0.01 | 0.01086 | 0.00109 | 0.00014 | 0.00003 |

| 0.30 | 0.04686 | 0.02088 | 0.00579 | 0.00321 |

Roughly halves per 4× sims (consistent with 1/√N averaging). No plateau; heads to 0.

### policy-frozen — visit-argmax plateaus, but Q-argmax is corrected (values exact)

visit-argmax:

| ε \ sims | 30 | 100 | 400 | 1600 |
|----------|----|-----|-----|------|
| 0.01 | 0.00076 | 0.00090 | 0.00094 | 0.00092 |
| 0.03 | 0.00549 | 0.00530 | 0.00530 | 0.00468 |
| 0.10 | 0.02106 | 0.02107 | 0.02030 | 0.01978 |
| 0.30 | 0.05239 | 0.05236 | 0.05220 | 0.04531 |

Q-argmax (same runs):

| ε \ sims | 30 | 100 | 400 | 1600 |
|----------|----|-----|-----|------|
| 0.01 | 0.00265 | 0.00203 | 0.00167 | **0.00000** |
| 0.03 | 0.00266 | 0.00277 | 0.00280 | **0.00073** |
| 0.10 | 0.00389 | 0.00332 | 0.00334 | **0.00312** |
| 0.30 | 0.01090 | 0.00945 | 0.00662 | **0.00579** |

The tree's **values are recovered** (Q-argmax ≪ visit-argmax and improves with sims),
but **visit-count selection stays prior-captured**: with exact frontier values a
confidently-wrong sharp prior still wins the visit count, because bearoff move-gaps
(~0.01-0.03) are far below the cpuct=2.0 exploration bonus, so the mis-primed action
never gives up its visit lead within 1600 sims. Mild policy error (ε=0.01) is cheap
for *both* rules.

### both-frozen — value bias sets the floor; policy adds on top

visit-argmax tracks value-frozen (value bias dominates the floor); Q-argmax is higher
than value-frozen's Q (policy error adds a visit/prior penalty that Q partly dodges).

|----------|----|-----|-----|------|----------|
| 0.01 | 0.00101 | 0.00097 | 0.00100 | 0.00093 | 0.00127 |
| 0.03 | 0.00557 | 0.00536 | 0.00518 | 0.00476 | 0.00501 |

## The four predictions vs the data

1. **Fresh = variance, averaged out** — ✅ **HOLDS.** Monotone decrease to ~0, ~1/√N.
2. **Frozen = bias, plateaus at the 1-ply anchor** — ✅ **HOLDS, textbook.** Every ε
   converges to its zero-sim anchor and stops; sims cannot beat the frontier bias.
3. **Policy error is cheap** — ⚠️ **HOLDS FOR THE VALUES, with a selection caveat.**
   Q-argmax corrects policy error with sims (as predicted). But the *practical* move
   (visit-argmax) is **not** corrected under a sharp confidently-wrong prior when
   move-gaps ≪ cpuct scale — visits stay prior-captured. Mild policy error is cheap
   for both rules. This is the sharpest new finding and refines the sweep's
   "few sims can't resolve ~0.01 gaps" into "a *wrong sharp prior* + tiny gaps defeats
   visit-count selection at any realistic budget, even though Q is right."
4. **NN: sims fix variance+policy, only depth fixes value bias** — ✅ **HOLDS** (Rung 7).

## Rung 7 — the real race NN on the curve

Checkpoint `race_iter_50.data` (256w×5b race net). NN value convention matches
`eval_bearoff_accuracy.jl` (value on the /3 normalized scale). All values raw points.

**NN value error vs exact table** on the 3443 frontier children (post-move states the
evaluator would score):

| where | MAE | RMSE | bias |
|-------|-----|------|------|
| frontier children | 0.693 | **1.015** | **+0.594** |
| root decision states | 0.191 | 0.500 | −0.037 |

The frontier bias (+0.59) is **large** and specific to the frontier: the children are
mostly the opponent's **pre-dice chance nodes** (76% non-doubles), which are
**out-of-distribution** for a net trained on post-dice decision nodes. On in-
distribution root decision states the error is small (MAE 0.19, ~0 bias).

**Sibling-error structure** (the expected wrinkle): decompose each child error
`eᵢ = c_root + dᵢ`.

- total error variance 0.0753, **common (across-root) variance 0.0713 → frac_common = 0.947**.
- **94.7% of the NN's value error is a common per-position shift** that **cancels in
  argmax** over siblings. The regret-driving **differential (within-root) RMSE is only
  0.263** raw points — vs a raw RMSE of 1.015.

**Predicted vs actual regret @600 sims** (NN-as-evaluator = apples-to-apples with the
frozen-value curve: uniform root priors, frontier = NN):

| quantity | regret |
|----------|--------|
| predicted from frozen curve at ε = **diffRMSE 0.263** (in-grid interp) | 0.0495 |
| predicted at ε = total RMSE 1.015 (off-grid, clamped to ε=0.3 — unreliable) | 0.0532 |
| **actual NN-as-evaluator @600** | **0.0660** (P(wrong)=61.5%) |
| actual/pred(diffRMSE) | **1.34×** |

The **valid** prediction is the diff-RMSE one (0.263 is in-grid *and* is the true
regret-driver given 94.7% sibling correlation). Actual (0.066) is **1.34× the
prediction — within the ~2× validation band.** The residual (actual slightly above)
is because the NN's differential errors are *structured / heavier-tailed* than iid
Gaussian noise of the same RMSE (it mis-ranks specific bearoff shapes, not randomly).
Predicting from the raw RMSE (1.015) would be wildly wrong without the sibling-
correlation correction — confirming the wrinkle: **sibling correlation makes real
regret far lower than the naive iid-RMSE prediction.**

**The mode matters enormously:**

| NN usage @600 sims | regret | P(wrong) |
|--------------------|--------|----------|
| NN-as-evaluator at bearoff frontier (queries OOD pre-dice children) | 0.0660 | 61.5% |
| raw NN 1-ply (zero search, also OOD children) | 0.0607 | 55.7% |
| **NN-as-oracle** (no bearoff eval; passthrough keeps NN on in-distribution post-dice decision nodes) | **0.0015** | 17.5% |

NN-as-evaluator ≈ NN-1-ply (0.066 ≈ 0.061): search **barely helps** because the
frontier bias is unfixable by iterations (predictions 2 & 4). NN-as-oracle is **44×
better** (0.0015) — it queries the NN only on in-distribution states, its common
errors cancel in argmax, and search on those good values works. This model is
**near-optimal** on bearoff in its real self-play usage.

## Practical rules that fall out

1. **When do sims help?** Sims correct **value variance** (fresh noise → 0 as ~1/√N)
   and recover **policy error in Q**. Sims do **not** correct **value bias** at the
   frontier (plateau at the 1-ply anchor), and do **not** rescue **visit-count
   selection** from a confidently-wrong sharp prior when move-gaps ≪ cpuct.

2. **What does depth-1 frontier bias cost?** Exactly the 1-ply regret at that bias
   level — e.g. a frozen ε=0.1 raw-pt value error costs ~0.023 regret no matter the
   budget. A stronger evaluator (lower frontier bias) is worth more than more sims
   once past the ~400-sim knee.

3. **Bearoff-evaluator cutoff policy — do NOT use the NN as a frontier evaluator on
   bearoff/pre-dice nodes.** The NN is OOD on opponent pre-dice chance nodes (+0.59
   bias, 0.066 regret, ≈ its 1-ply level — search can't fix it). Use the **exact k=7
   table** as the cutoff (regret ~0, and this is why the table cutoff exists), or, if
   no table, prefer **NN-as-oracle with passthrough** (keeps the NN on in-distribution
   post-dice decision nodes → 0.0015 regret). Never argmax the NN over pre-dice
   children (1-ply or evaluator mode).

4. **Sibling-error correlation is your friend and must be measured.** Raw value
   RMSE/bias massively *overstates* move error: 94.7% of this NN's error is a common
   shift that cancels in argmax. Track the **differential (within-root) RMSE**, not the
   raw RMSE, as the move-quality-relevant error metric (raw RMSE 1.015 → effective
   0.263). A value-accuracy eval that reports only MAE/RMSE/bias will look alarming
   while the model plays nearly perfectly.

5. **Progressive sim schedules.** With exact/strong values, dropping below ~100-400
   sims re-introduces allocation regret (baseline 30-sim = 0.010, 100 = 0.001).
   Early-training schedules may safely use 100-200 sims *only* once the evaluator is
   strong; with a weak/biased evaluator, extra sims are wasted (they cannot beat the
   frontier bias) — spend the compute on a better evaluator or on depth past the
   frontier instead.

6. **Temperature-0 move selection is prior-fragile in low-gap regimes.** With sharp
   priors and ~0.01-0.03 move-gaps, visit-count argmax can lock onto a mis-primed
   move while Q is correct. For bearoff/race eval where gaps are tiny, selecting by
   **Q (or lower cpuct / higher visit budget)** is safer than raw visit-count.
